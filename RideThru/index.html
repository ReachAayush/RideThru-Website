Concept

RideThru transforms a dull stationary bike workout into a virtual reality experience that will make exercising be more fun. By attaching sensors to an exercise bike and integrating them with an Oculus Rift, a user can experience biking through all sorts of virtual terrains and even play video games with the bike as a controller.

Motivation

Stationary bikes are a very popular indoor exercise choice. Many people would prefer outdoor biking but due to weather, busy schedules, or other reasons, stationary bike exercises become the next best workout. The most predominant issue with them is that they are extremeley boring since they are not actually moving anywhere. Furthermore, if people don't enjoy some workout, they are less likely to continue working out. Ride through will make this type of workout fun by allowing users to play games or ride through new terrains in virtual reality. Ultimately this will encourage people to stay healthy as much as possible.

Competition

PaperDude VR

Virtual reality paperboy simulation

  • Implemented using physical bike
  • Also uses Oculus for Virtual Reality.
  • Different use context. (Not made for exercise)
  • Limited to just one game
  • Utilizes Kinect to track bodily movements as well

Expresso

Interactive bike workout machine. Simulates new environment by showing it on screen

  • Display not as immersive as Oculus Rift
  • Does have support for multiplayer
  • Extremely expensive, starting at $4000

Specifications

Sensors and Hardware

  • RPM Sensors
  • Motor Controlled Biking Resistance
  • Bodily Sensors(heart rate, temperature, etc)

User Experience and Interaction

  • Oculus Rift Dev Kit 2
  • Electronic Handlebar for Turning
  • Adjustable speed fan

System Communication

  • Raspberry Pi Model B+
  • Custom PCB

Requirements

Functional Requirements

  • Render VR environment at at least 70fps
  • Accurately measure RPM so speed in VR can be adjusted
  • Have virtual reality game/terrain accept handlebars as input
  • Have fan power drivin by speed to simulate wind
  • Oculus Rift headset stays on despite sweat

Non Functional Requirements

  • Effectivity- Delay of under 5ms of rendering new display after user turns head
  • Usability- User should be able to control everything about the bike even with the Oculus headset on
  • Reliability- Sensors should not fall off or get ruined despite the fact that they will be under significant stress from the moving parts of the bike
